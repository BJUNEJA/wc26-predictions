The forecasting tournament

One table, one metric, one rule: publish before kickoff, score against reality. The champion is the site's production model. Every other row is a challenger: a different model, a different method, or a different source of intelligence. All scored on the same Brier scale, on the same matches. The question the leaderboard answers: does any signal, from any source, forecast better than the Elo ensemble?

The champion-challenger framework

In production analytics, a champion is the model currently making decisions; a challenger is any candidate replacement that runs in parallel, scored on the same outcomes, but not yet trusted with decisions. The champion holds its position until a challenger demonstrates statistically significant improvement over a sustained evaluation window, not a lucky streak. This framework is standard practice in credit scoring (OCC Bulletin 2011-12), clinical trials (where it maps onto non-inferiority designs), and recommendation systems (A/B testing). Its core discipline: the challenger must beat the champion on the champion's turf, with the champion's metrics, over the champion's evaluation period. Selection bias (submitting only your best backtest) and survivorship bias (dropping challengers that start poorly) are the two threats the framework exists to prevent.

References: Provost, F. and Fawcett, T. (2013), Data Science for Business, O'Reilly, ch. 11 (champion-challenger in credit scoring). Berry, S. M. et al. (2011), Bayesian Adaptive Methods for Clinical Trials, CRC Press (adaptive challenger designs). Kohavi, R., Tang, D. and Xu, Y. (2020), Trustworthy Online Controlled Experiments: A Practical Guide to A/B Testing, Cambridge University Press (the tech-industry formulation). For the forecasting-tournament lineage specifically: Tetlock, P. E. and Gardner, D. How to enter a challenger

Any model, any method, any person. Three requirements: (1) register before your first prediction is scored, (2) submit a full probability vector (home/draw/away summing to 1) for every match you call, (3) all predictions submitted before kickoff. Selection bias is the enemy: you may not backtest on scored matches and submit only winners. Late entry is permitted; the "Scored" column shows how many matches each model has been evaluated on.

python python/model_register.py your_id "Model Name" "Your Name" "One-line method"
python python/model_predict.py your_id "Home" "Away" 0.45 0.28 0.27
Or batch: python python/model_predict.py your_id --file your_predictions.csv
