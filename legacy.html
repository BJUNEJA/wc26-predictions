2014, Brazil: the knockout run

Published match by match at ubanalytics.wordpress.com as the tournament unfolded. 13 of 16 knockout outcomes correct (81.2%), 7 with the exact goal difference and another 4 within one goal, including the final: Germany over Argentina by a goal difference of 1, which is precisely how it ended. For context, FiveThirtyEight's interactive missed that final.

2018, Russia: the honest middle chapter

A larger experiment: 53 published predictions spanning the group stage and knockouts, 30 correct (56.6%); 24/43 in the group stage and 6/10 in the knockouts. The instructive failure: the model was binary and 8 misses were draws it structurally could not predict. That lesson is why the 2026 system carries a Poisson scoreline model whose entire job is the draw.

What carried forward

Three constants across twelve years: predictions published before kickoff, results posted next to them whatever they say, and a team-strength-difference architecture under the hood. What changed is the discipline around it: 2026 adds strictly out-of-sample evaluation, calibrated probabilities, draw modeling, and a scorecard that updates itself. The archive of the first two campaigns remains at ubanalytics.wordpress.com.
